## ROUND 3: Addressing Our Specific Vulnerabilities

### Vulnerability #1: Stock-Bond Correlation Breakdown

**üèóÔ∏è Infrastructure:**

The 2022 correlation crisis proved infrastructure's case definitively. When stock-bond correlation flipped from -0.5 to +0.5, infrastructure maintained its 0.3-0.5 correlation to equities *and* delivered positive absolute returns. This isn't smoothed accounting‚ÄîEDHECinfra uses transaction-based pricing. Our research shows infrastructure captures only 30% of equity downside historically. The correlation is low because cash flows are driven by contracts and regulation, not market sentiment. This is *structural* diversification.

**üí∞ Private Credit:**

Private credit's 0.4-0.6 correlation is moderate but honest‚Äîno appraisal games. More importantly, floating-rate structures have *negative duration*, providing natural offset to bond portfolio losses when rates rise. Traditional bonds lost 13% in 2022 precisely because duration worked against them. Our yields *increase* during tightening cycles. Yes, PE-backed borrower concentration creates indirect equity exposure, but senior secured positioning (65% average recovery rates) provides meaningful downside protection through credit structures.

### Vulnerability #2: Inflation Protection

**üèóÔ∏è Infrastructure:**

**üí∞ Private Credit:**
